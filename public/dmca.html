Copyright Takedown Policy (DMCA)

Effective date: 20 May 2026 · Last updated: 20 May 2026

1. Overview

Brand Gita respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects users of the Services to do the same. This Copyright Takedown Policy explains how to submit a notice if you believe content published via Brand Gita infringes your copyright, and how the process works.

This Policy is modelled on the notice-and-takedown procedure of the United States Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. §512) ("DMCA") and applies to takedown requests from rights holders globally.

2. Important: How Brand Gita Works

Before submitting a notice, please understand how the Services function:

Brand Gita is a publishing tool — it allows users to create and publish content to their own social media accounts (Instagram, YouTube, Facebook). Brand Gita does not host, store, or serve user-published content. Content published via Brand Gita resides on the relevant social media platform, not on Brand Gita's servers.

This means:

  • For content published to Instagram or Facebook: Contact Meta directly at https://www.facebook.com/help/contact/1758255661104383 or use Instagram's reporting tools
  • For content published to YouTube: Use YouTube's copyright complaint process at https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/2807622
  • For content appearing on brandgita.com itself: Submit a notice to me using the process below

If the infringing content is on a third-party platform, contacting that platform directly is faster and more effective than contacting me.

3. Submitting a Takedown Notice

If you believe that content on the brandgita.com website infringes your copyright, submit a written notice to:

Email: support@brandgita.com
Subject line: "DMCA Takedown Notice"

Your notice must include all of the following to be valid:

  1. Your identity: Your full legal name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work: A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed, or if multiple works are covered, a representative list
  3. Identification of the infringing material: A description of the material you claim is infringing, with enough detail for me to locate it — ideally including the specific URL(s) on brandgita.com where the material appears
  4. Good-faith belief statement: A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  5. Accuracy statement: A statement that the information in the notice is accurate
  6. Authorisation statement: A statement, made under penalty of perjury (if you are in a jurisdiction where this applies), that you are the copyright owner or are authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner
  7. Signature: Your physical or electronic signature

Incomplete notices may not be actioned. I reserve the right to request additional information.

4. What Happens After I Receive a Notice

Upon receiving a valid takedown notice:

  1. I will review the notice promptly — typically within 10 business days
  2. If the notice is valid and the material is on brandgita.com, I will remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material
  3. I will notify the user who posted the material (where applicable) that it has been removed
  4. I will retain the notice for record-keeping purposes

I am not able to remove content from third-party platforms (Instagram, YouTube, Facebook) — you must contact those platforms directly.

5. Counter-Notice

If your content was removed in response to a takedown notice and you believe the removal was in error or that you have the right to use the material, you may submit a counter-notice to support@brandgita.com with the subject line "DMCA Counter-Notice."

Your counter-notice must include:

  1. Your full legal name, address, telephone number, and email address
  2. Identification of the material that was removed and the location where it appeared before removal
  3. A statement, under penalty of perjury (where applicable), that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification
  4. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the courts of New Zealand (and, if you are in the United States, the federal district court for your district) for any legal action relating to the notice
  5. Your physical or electronic signature

Upon receiving a valid counter-notice, I will review it and may, at my discretion, restore the removed material after the requisite waiting period unless the original complainant files a court action.

6. Repeat Infringers

It is my policy to terminate the accounts of users who are found to be repeat infringers of intellectual property rights. Determinations of repeat infringement are made at my sole discretion.

7. Abuse of This Process

Submitting a takedown notice with knowledge that the material is not infringing may result in liability for damages, including legal costs, under applicable law. Please ensure your notice is accurate and made in good faith.

8. Other IP Concerns

For trademark infringement, defamation, or other intellectual property concerns unrelated to copyright, please contact support@brandgita.com with a description of your concern. I will review all legitimate IP complaints promptly.
